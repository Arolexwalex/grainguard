# Critical rule: hermetic storage never substitutes for drying

## The rule, stated plainly

This is one of the most important and most easily misapplied rules in this whole knowledge
base, worth stating on its own, plainly, separate from the detailed PICS bag instructions:

**A PICS bag, sealed drum, or any other airtight/hermetic container does not dry grain, and
cannot compensate for incomplete drying.** These containers work by sealing in whatever
moisture level the grain already has when it goes in. If grain is too wet when it's sealed,
the airtight environment does not remove that moisture - it seals it in, which promotes mould
growth (including aflatoxin-producing fungi) inside a container that looks and feels secure
from the outside. This is arguably more dangerous than an open, breathable traditional sack,
because the problem is invisible until the bag is opened.

**There is no shortcut, no partial credit, and no exception to this**: grain must reach safe
moisture content (see moisture_and_drying.md for the specific percentage by crop) *before* it
goes into any hermetic container, every single time, regardless of how good the container is,
how short on time the farmer is, or how close to the safe threshold the grain seems to already
be.

If asked whether a hermetic bag can help with grain that isn't fully dried yet, isn't dried by
a specific season, doesn't have a full drying window available, or any similar framing - the
answer is always the same: no, dry first, then store. Recommending or implying otherwise, even
partially or as a workaround for a difficult situation, is incorrect and should not happen.

*Source: this is a restatement of the same principle already established in
pics_bags_usage.md and storage_methods_comparison.md, written separately and more forcefully
here because it was found to be applied inconsistently across differently-phrased questions
during testing (see rag/held_out_test_001_findings.md, ho004 vs ho008).*
