# Nigeria-specific storage technologies (NSPRI)

The Nigerian Stored Products Research Institute (NSPRI) is the federal government body
specifically mandated to research and reduce postharvest losses in Nigeria, with zonal offices
in Ibadan, Kano, Lagos, Port-Harcourt, and outstations in Sapele and Maiduguri. Their
technologies are designed and tested specifically for Nigerian farming conditions, which makes
them a strong locally-grounded complement to the IITA and Ghana material already in this
knowledge base.

## Notable NSPRI technologies relevant to maize and groundnut

**Maize crib** — a raised, ventilated storage structure designed specifically for unshelled
(on-the-cob) maize. Airflow through the structure helps continue drying the cob after harvest
and discourages mould growth, making it a good option where grain has been harvested slightly
before full field-drying is complete.

**Improved hermetic storage (household level)** — NSPRI-adapted airtight bag/container systems
similar in principle to PICS bags, sized for individual household use rather than bulk
warehouse storage.

**Solar dryer** — a low-cost structure that uses trapped solar heat to accelerate drying beyond
what open sun-drying on a tarpaulin achieves, useful in the humid southern zones where ambient
drying is slower and mould risk during the drying window is higher.

**Evaporative cooling chamber** — primarily used for perishables rather than grain, but relevant
if this project later expands beyond maize/groundnut into vegetables or fruit storage advice.

## Why this matters for local relevance
NSPRI has specifically prioritised designing training materials in local languages to reach
farmers with limited formal literacy, and works through an "adopted village" demonstration
model — testing technologies directly with farming communities and gathering feedback before
wider rollout. This reinforces that the real bottleneck matches what we identified earlier:
proven technology exists, but reaching farmers directly, in their own language, at the right
moment, is the unsolved part.
